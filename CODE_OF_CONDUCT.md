# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

MediGuard is intended to be a respectful, professional, and constructive collaboration space. Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to help create an environment where people can participate safely, communicate clearly, and do strong work together.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate with respect and professionalism.
- Give direct technical feedback without making it personal.
- Share context clearly so others can review and collaborate effectively.
- Assume good intent while staying honest about risks and issues.
- Be considerate when discussing reliability, healthcare operations, and sensitive workflows.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or threats
- Personal attacks, insults, or degrading language
- Deliberate disruption, trolling, or bad-faith participation
- Publishing private, confidential, or security-sensitive information without permission
- Repeated refusal to follow project boundaries or maintainer direction

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, review threads, and any project-linked spaces where people participate as part of the MediGuard community.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct. Repeated or severe violations may result in temporary or permanent participation restrictions.

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it through the private path described in [SUPPORT.md](SUPPORT.md). Reports will be reviewed respectfully and handled as appropriately as possible.
